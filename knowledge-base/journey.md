# My journey

I started in Computer Science & Engineering at CMR College of Engineering & Technology in
Hyderabad, India (Nov 2020 – Apr 2024), in the Honours track. I graduated First Class with
Distinction at 9.06 / 10.0. That's where the fundamentals set in — algorithms, systems, and a
competitive-programming habit I still keep up (5★ on HackerRank, 4★ on CodeChef, and a 255-day
GeeksforGeeks problem-of-the-day streak).

While I was an undergrad I took my first applied roles. In 2022 I interned as a Data Scientist at
Swecha, building Telugu speech-recognition pipelines and containerizing NLP models with Docker for
an underserved-language community. In 2023 I did a Data Analytics internship through AICTE
EduSkills with AWS Academy, building end-to-end cloud pipelines on S3, Glue, and Athena. Those two
experiences pushed me from "models in a notebook" toward "systems that run somewhere."

In August 2024 I moved to the United States for a Master's in Data Science & Artificial
Intelligence at the University of Central Missouri, finishing in May 2026 with a 4.0 GPA and the
Graduate Student Achievement Award. Alongside coursework I worked as a Graduate Laboratory
Assistant, teaching 40+ students per semester the same end-to-end ML workflow I care about —
ingestion through evaluation.

Grad school is also where my focus sharpened into **AI engineering**: LLM and multi-agent systems,
retrieval, and the MLOps to ship them. I built a benchmarked RAG research agent, a five-agent
Microsoft Agent Framework platform with MCP-grounded citations, a full MLOps fraud-detection
pipeline, and an injection-safe vision-language claim adjudicator that placed in the top 1% at a
HackerRank hackathon. The through-line across all of it is rigor — I don't trust a model I haven't
benchmarked, and I care as much about evaluation and deployment as I do about the model itself.
